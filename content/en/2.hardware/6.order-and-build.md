# Order and Build

This guide is meant to be practical for the current repository state. It assumes you want to fabricate the mainboard, optionally use PCBA, and then do first bring-up on the bench.

## Files You Need

For a typical PCB or PCBA order, use:

- [Schematic PDF](/keero/downloads/keero_mainboard_schematic.pdf)
- [PCB layout PDF](/keero/downloads/PCB_board_2026-04-02.pdf)
- [BOM CSV](/keero/downloads/mainboard-bom.csv)
- [Pick and place CSV](/keero/downloads/mainboard-pick-place.csv)
- `keero-hardware/mainboard/fabrication/gerbers/*`

For design review and mechanical checks, also use:

- `/keero/3d/mainboard.step`
- `/keero/3d/*.STL`
- PCB render images in `/keero/images/`

## Suggested Ordering Workflow

1. Review the schematic and board PDFs first.
2. Upload the gerbers for PCB fabrication.
3. Upload the BOM and pick-place files if using assembly.
4. Double-check that all FPC connectors, headers, switches, and module-specific parts are available from the selected assembler.
5. Compare the assembled preview against the PCB render images before approving production.

## Parts Worth Double-Checking Before PCBA

These are the components most likely to matter during sourcing and assembly review:

- `U1` `AXP2101`
- `U2` `ESP32-S3-WROOM-1U-N16R8`
- `U3` `MAX98357AETE+T`
- `U4` `LIS2DW12TR`
- `U5` `ICS-41350`
- `U6` `DRV2605LDGSR`
- `U7` `SX1308`
- `CN2` camera FPC connector
- `CN5` OLED FPC connector
- `USB1` USB-C connector

Why these deserve attention:

- QFN, TQFN, VSSOP, and LGA packages are much less forgiving than passives
- the ESP32 module and PMIC are critical path parts
- FPC connector orientation mistakes are easy to make during review
- microphone, amplifier, and haptic paths are sensitive to both placement and routing assumptions

## Recommended First Build Strategy

For a safer first run:

- let PCBA populate all fine-pitch ICs and passives
- manually install only optional or experiment-facing connectors if needed
- verify camera and OLED mating direction before ordering matching parts
- keep at least one board for pure bring-up and one for enclosure/mechanical testing

## Bench Bring-Up Checklist

1. Inspect the board under magnification, especially around `U1`, `U2`, `U3`, `U4`, `U5`, `U6`, `U7`, `CN2`, and `CN5`.
2. Check for shorts between power and ground before connecting a battery.
3. Power the board through USB-C first, not battery first.
4. Confirm that the charge LED and PMIC behavior look sane.
5. Verify that the ESP32 enumerates over USB or at least reaches a programmable state.
6. Confirm the expected rails are present:
   - `VSYS`
   - `3V3A`
   - `3V3B`
   - `2V8A`
   - `1V5A`
   - `VPP`
7. Only after power looks stable, connect camera, OLED, speaker, or external modules.

## Assembly Risk Areas

### Power section

The PMIC and boost sections are the highest-risk functional blocks because a mistake here can make all downstream debugging misleading.

Check especially:

- `AXP2101` orientation
- inductor placements `L1`, `L2`, `L3`, `L4`
- diode orientation for `D4`
- USB protection part `D5`

### FPC connectors

`CN2` and `CN5` should be visually checked against the mating cable direction and lock orientation.

### Audio and haptics

If the board powers correctly but output peripherals behave strangely, inspect:

- `MAX98357A`
- `DRV2605L`
- speaker and motor connector polarity

## What To Test After Basic Power-Up

- USB programming path
- boot and reset buttons
- PMIC IRQ handling
- accelerometer detection over I2C
- microphone clock/data activity
- I2S speaker amplifier output
- haptic motor output
- display power boost section
- camera interface continuity

## Current Firmware Reality

The full Keero firmware is still coming soon, but the existing `tracks` firmware already gives a useful style reference for:

- PlatformIO layout
- GPIO-level motor control
- battery voltage supervision
- basic status lighting

That means a reasonable bring-up flow is:

1. validate hardware first
2. bring up USB and PMIC
3. verify GPIO, I2C, and I2S one subsystem at a time
4. only then layer higher-level firmware and AI features
