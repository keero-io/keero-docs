# Power and Signals

## Input Sources

The board accepts power from:

- `USB1` over USB-C
- `CN1` battery connector
- `J1` pogo interface for docking and charging use cases

## PMIC Section

The central power manager is `U1` (`AXP2101`), supported by:

- inductors `L1`, `L2`, `L3`
- multiple bulk and decoupling capacitors
- charge indicator LED `LED1`
- `PWRON` switch `SW1`

Named outputs visible in the schematic:

- `VSYS`
- `VBUS`
- `3V3A`
- `3V3B`
- `2V8A`
- `1V5A`
- `DV_2.8V`
- `DV_1.5V`
- `VPP`
- `VPPA`

This is a strong hint that the design intentionally separates rails for compute, camera, display, and mixed-signal sections instead of running everything from one flat `3V3`.

## Display Boost Converter

The OLED power section uses:

- `U7`: `SX1308`
- `L4`: `4.7uH`
- `D4`: `SS14`
- divider resistors `R18` (`620k`) and `R19` (`30k`)

The schematic labels this block as `OLED_Power`, and the resulting boosted rail is routed as `VPP`.

## Practical Read of the Design

From the rail naming and connected ICs, the current board looks optimized for:

- one battery-powered portable core board
- camera plus display operation
- digital audio input and output
- responsive haptic feedback
- future external modules through headers and pogo

That lines up well with the project goal of an interactive AI device rather than a simple microcontroller dev board.
